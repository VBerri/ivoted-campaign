Why Voting Matters in American Law

Voting rights have been central to American democracy. The **Voting Rights Act of 1965** aimed to eliminate racial discrimination in voting, ensuring every citizen could exercise their right to vote. Other significant laws include:

  • 15th Amendment (1870): Prohibits denying the right to vote based on race or color.
  • 19th Amendment (1920): Grants women the right to vote, expanding voting rights to all citizens.
  • 26th Amendment (1971): Lowers the voting age from 21 to 18, giving young people a voice in government.

These laws underscore the belief that every citizen’s voice matters in shaping the country's future. Voting is not only a right but a civic duty.
